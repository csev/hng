Select the Airport Utility

Hopefully your system will find the base station. If you cannot find the base station, check the Airport documentation on how to perform complete hardware resets.

If you cannot find the base station after a complete reset, recheck the installation in your computer and the software in your computer.

Base stations have a password - this keeps your neighbors from using the system and setting your parameters.

If you forget the password follow the hardware reset procedure in the Airport documentation.

This screen sets the identity of your wireless base station. The Severance_Home network name will be used to distinguish among different Airport wireless networks.

In the TCP/IP configuration section, you are defining how the Airport will connect to the "non-wireless" network. These settings should be as if the Airport was just another client on the "non-wireless" network.

In another example we will see configurations other than "Manually" - other options include DHCP and PPP.

This sets the wireless configuration. If you enable encryption, your system will be more secure, but you will not be able to interoperate with a wide variety of non-Apple equipment.

Once you turn on encryption, you will have to enter a password each time your client systems are powered on or woke up after a suspend.

Optimize placement deals with multiple base stations and can generally be ignored.
